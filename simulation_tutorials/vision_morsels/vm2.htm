USIP Online Simulation Creation Vision Morsels

     

Vision Morsel 2: The Big Bite

In this morsel we will discuss (at a very high level) the overall flow of creating, running and sharing simulations using the USIP Online Simulation Platform (USIP OSCW).

Think

This tool, or really any tool to aid instruction, pre-supposes that the person using it has something meaningful to teach. We can't really help you with this first step, but we have faith that you and many other fine people will come forward with lessons they want to teach. (We will describe some possible lessons in future morsels.)

Create

The next step is to use the wizard to create a simulation.

This can be broken down into these five steps:

  1. Enter Background Material - The 'Simulation Creator' (SC) will use the wizard to enter the background materials that the players will need to read.
  2. Define Phases - Here the SC will consider how he or she envisions the simulation progressing toward the end and define a phase for each stage of play. This may be as simple as defining one phase for when the simulation is in progress, and one for when the simulation is completed: 'in progress' and 'done.'
  3. Create Actors - The SC will enter information particular to each Actor involved in the simulation.
  4. Create Actions and Views - The SC will enter in what actors can do and see. Many of these will involve what we call 'special features' which we will discuss in a future morsel.
  5. Assign Actions and Views to Actors - The SC will then assign the actions and views available to the different actors in the different phases of the simulation.

 

Play

The next step is to conduct the simulation with a group of players.

This can be broken down into the four steps below.

  1. Create Running Simulation - Give a specific name to a play session, for example 'Beta-test 1' or 'Summer 2009 Session 1.'
  2. Create Users - Each player will need a login account to enter into a simulation.
  3. Assign Users to Actors - Each player will need to be assigned to an actor. For example, the player 'John Doe' may be playing the actor 'The President.'
  4. Invite Players to Begin - This enables players to login to play the roles assigned to them and can (optionally) send them an email to let them know that the simulation has begun.

 

Share

One can stop at step 3 above (or more likely go from 'play' back to think'), but our sincere hope is that once educators have created good simulations they will want to share these with others.

  1. Package Simulation - Take a simulation and package it for transport.
  2. Download / Upload from Web Site - Post a simulation to a community web site where that simulation is relevant. For example, people creating simulations around environmental themes may all be posting to one site sponsored by members of the green community.
  3. Import Simulation - Take a simulation that someone else has created and import it onto your system. You may be able to (depending an parameters set by the original simulation creator) conduct, or even change then conduct, the simulation with players of your choosing.
